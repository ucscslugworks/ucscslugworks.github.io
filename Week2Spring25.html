This week, I spoke with Kelsey Wong, a Second Year Psychology major, about a creative project she worked on at Slugworks. She designed custom Pokémon cards as gifts for her fellowship group in the Asian American Christian Fellowship (AACF).

Q: What inspired this project?

A: We wanted to make something special for our fellowship brothers, so we designed Pokémon-style cards for them. Each card has their name, a custom move based on their personality, and their HP, which is actually their birthday. We also added their graduation year and current year as part of the stats. It is a fun and personalized way to celebrate everyone.

Q: How did you create the cards?
A: We used an online Pokémon card generator to design them and I hand-drew some of the graphics using Procreate on my tablet. The biggest challenge was that the file size got corrupted, so the image resolution was not right. We had to manually adjust and re-export them to get everything looking good.

Q: How did you get involved with Slugworks?
A: One of our friends, Luke, is an engineering major and he is always talking about the cool things he makes here. When we were brainstorming gift ideas, he suggested using Slugworks to print them and use the cricut maker 3.

Q: Are you working on any other projects at Slugworks?
A: Not at the moment, but we have made stickers for our fellowship before and I would love to explore more creative projects here. I also heard about workshops where clubs can come in and learn to make something together, which sounds really fun.

Have you ever wondered what it’s like to teach your own class? Now’s your chance to become a student group tutor!

Join CMPM 115: Lead by Design, keep your potential topic in mind! Through this 5-unit course, upper division students with a range of technical skill sets they are passionate about sharing will be guided through the process of developing a 3 unit engineering project-based design lab. You will then be paid to teach the course to lower division students in a subsequent quarter. Lead by Design requires a team-based application, and is tailored to upper division students.

Applications are now open for Fall Quarter! Positions are approved on a rolling basis.
