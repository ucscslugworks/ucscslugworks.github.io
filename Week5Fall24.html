Slugworks Student Spotlight

This week, I spoke with Dante Massaro, a 3rd Year Robotics Engineering Major about his involvement with Slugworks and the projects he’s completed.


Q: How did you get involved in Slugworks?

A: My first interaction with Slugworks was when I became part of the staff. I’m buddies with Ishan, who was heavily involved with the S-Lab, so I always knew about the space and the transition from S-Lab to Slugworks. I had seen the lab before I started working here so when I saw the application on Handshake, I thought it was cool and decided to apply. I've been involved with Slugworks since the Fall quarter of 2023. I've always had an interest in working in machine shops, starting back in high school and even earlier when I was a kid. I found the opportunity to work at Slugworks and what I thought would be "cool" turned out to be a fantastic experience.

Q: Which of the projects that you’ve worked on is your favorite?

A: My favorite project so far isn’t really anything big or flashy, but it’s been incredibly rewarding. I’ve been working on organizing the tool chests and improving the ergonomics of the workspace. There were random objects scattered around the shop, and we turned that "mess" into a professional-looking, well-organized setup. It was really satisfying to bring order to the space and make it more functional.

Q: Do you have any advice for someone that’s interested in getting involved?

A: Don’t hesitate —just come down to the basement and get started. The staff is super friendly, and there’s no barrier to entry, even if you don’t know how to use the machines. It’s super easy to be resourceful. Just walk into the shop, do the walkthrough, and start learning. If you don’t know the process for getting access to the shop, just come down and ask! Everyone is very approachable, down to earth, and happy to help.

Q: What skills have you gained since working at Slugworks?

A: Communication skills. One thing I didn’t initially expect when I got this job was how much it involves working with people. A lot of the job is onboarding, answering questions, and helping new students and members get familiar with the shop. Working with such a large group of people has really helped me develop communication skills. Many of the other members have a lot of technical experience, so it’s very easy to learn from the individuals working and staffing the shop.

Q: How do you think your experience at Slugworks has shaped your approach to engineering?
A: It hasn’t really shaped my approach to engineering yet, because I’m not fully at that level in the academic curriculum. However, my past experiences in professional shops have influenced what I bring to Slugworks. I use techniques and knowledge from those experiences to make Slugworks more ergonomic and efficient. For example, I’ve worked on building a steel bumper for my Jeep, making a roof rack for surfboards, and working on a bike, all utilizing tools and knowledge from previous engineering experience. It’s similar to other machine shops where you can use the tools for whatever project you’re working on. A lot of it is about figuring out the entire approach to machining, not just the technical part but the strategy behind it.

Q: What are you most excited about for the future of Slugworks?
A: I’m really excited to see the shop develop into a more professional space. In the future, I want to see more professional looking workspaces, tool setups, and dedicated areas like stock storage and a welding zone. I envision a future where Slugworks has an awesome professional environment for getting things done.

Q: How do you think Slugworks contributes to the UCSC community?
A: I think it does a lot. First off, it enables our student Clubs and STEM teams to carry out their projects by providing professional facilities and tools. Capstone students also come in to work and complete their studies—ECE and Intro to Mech students often use the shop to build robots and work on their capstone projects. Another great thing about Slugworks is that it offers a space for all students, not just engineering majors, to come in and work on personal projects. I’ve seen students working on musical instruments, cars, bikes, hobbies—basically anything creative. It’s a liberating space where students can bring their ideas to life, regardless of their major. That’s an important contribution to the campus.

CMPM 115 (Lead by Design):

“One of the highlights of teaching the Design-Build Experience Course has been seeing people in their “aha” moments when they light up and figure something out. That is not something that you see in large engineering classes and lecture halls. It’s also been great to experience this new way of teaching with a more experimental topic, because cybersecurity isn’t really taught in schools yet. It’s been a positive experience for both the instructors and the students, and the students feel comfortable engaging and asking questions.” (Jacobs Taranenko, 5th year, 2024)

“I think that having classes that are focused on practical learning at the start of engineering education is very valuable and not as common as it should be. CMPM 17 (First Year Design):

“As an environmental science major, working with electrical circuitry in the engineering building is outside my field, but I’ve enjoyed it much more than I expected. The TAs provided great support and kept everyone feeling excited and comfortable, even when we faced challenges. At no point did I feel under-supported. We also had hands-on experiences in class, like going into the redwoods to collect mushrooms. Club Hub Breakdown!

At the heart of Slugworks is our Club Hub (BE-49), where a number of student organizations are hard at work building, innovating, and making things happen. Here are some of the incredible clubs you can get involved with:

Slugbotics :

Slugbotics is UCSC’s hub for hands-on robotics innovation. Whether it’s building underwater ROVs or competing in the MATE ROV Competition, this team dives into real-world challenges in robotics, offering students the chance to learn and create in a collaborative setting.


Formula Slug :

Rev up your passion for engineering with Formula Slug, where students design and build electric race cars to compete in the Formula SAE competitions. Whether you're into mechanics, electronics, or teamwork, this club is all about pushing the boundaries of automotive engineering.


Rocket Team :

UCSC Rocket Team is all about taking aerospace engineering to new heights. Competing in the prestigious NASA University Student Launch Initiative (USLI), the team designs and builds rockets that tackle real-world challenges in space exploration. With subteams focusing on vehicle design, payload innovation, and airbrakes for precision control, this club offers hands-on experience like no other.

Passion Meetups

There will be several passion meetups happening at Slugworks this quarter. Passion meetups are a space for students with similar interests to get together and talk about projects, share their interest, and meet new people. If you're interested in holding passion meetups, reach out to slugworks@ucsc.edu with your proposal.

Creative Code Collective: The Creative Code Collective will be having meetings every First and Third Monday of the Month at 7:30pm at BE-55 in Slugworks. Some past projects include:

- Interactive digital art using p5.js

- Music visualization with Perlin Noise

- Algorithmic music generation

- Shader coding for visual effects

- Generative art for jewelry design

- Simulating Minecraft-like environments

If you’re passionate about the intersection of art and technology, the Creative Code Collective is the perfect place to experiment, learn, and share your ideas!
