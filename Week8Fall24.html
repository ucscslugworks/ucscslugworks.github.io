This week, I spoke with Aethlyn Lim, a 1st Year Robotics Engineering Major about her involvement with Slugworks and the projects she’s completed. 

Q: How did you get involved in Slugworks?
A: I found Slugworks online while looking around for things to do before school started. I’m also part of Formula Slug, so that definitely helped me get started in Slugworks.

Q: What machines do you know how to use or work with the most? 
A: I’ve mainly worked with the 3D printers and laser cutters, but I also have a bit of experience with vacuum forming and CNC milling. 
Q: Which of the projects that you’ve worked on is your favorite?
A: My favorite project is the custom trophies I made for a robotics competition. Our organization was Gael Force Robotics, so I designed a laser cut and 3D printed trophy in the shape of a shield which had swords that could be pulled out. 
Q: Do you have any advice for someone who’s interested in getting involved?
A: Take the tests early. Just go through the Canvas modules and spend time on them—it’s good to get certified as soon as you can so you can get started on whatever projects you might want to work on. 
Q: What was the process for learning about the machines like?
A: For me, it was pretty straightforward because I already had a bit of experience with the machines I’ve gotten certified on so far. I just went through the Canvas modules and SOP materials, and that was enough to get me started. 
Q: What skills have you gained since working at Slugworks?
A: I’ve learned a lot about CAD. I only knew the basics from a class I took during COVID, but because I was bored I started exploring it on my own by just clicking random buttons, and learning what they did. This kind of gave me a lot of the skills I needed to start working on a couple personal projects. 
Q: Are there any future projects you’re excited to do?
A: I want to design a laser-cut puzzle box which fits different mechanisms in it in order to open it. 
Q: How do you think your experience at Slugworks has shaped your approach to engineering?
A: Working with CAD was a big challenge at first because I mostly learned by myself, and CAD has a steep learning curve. But once I got over that, it opened up a lot of creative possibilities, and I started CADing random things just for fun. 
Q: What CAD software are you most attuned with?
A: Fusion 360 and Onshape are the ones I use the most. 
Q: Would you like to see anything else down here in the maker space? Any specific machines or programs or meetups?
A: Personally, I haven’t seen a need for much else that I haven’t already found here, but it would be nice to have more materials like acrylic for the laser cutter. 
Q: What are you most excited about for the future of Formula Slug and Slugworks?
A: I’m excited to work on more personal projects. Back in high school, I could only do that during free periods or lunch, so it’s great to finally have a space where I can laser cut and work whenever I want. 
Q: How do you think Slugworks contributes to the UCSC community?
A: I think Slugworks really brings people together—artists, engineers, different clubs. It’s a space where everyone can work on projects that mean something to them, whether it’s personal projects or club-related. It creates a unique, collaborative environment at UCSC.

New! Enroll in Winter Design Build Classes!

CMPM 17-01: Creative Design in Virtual Reality: 

- Learn the basics of 3D application design for VR, using tools like ShapesXR and Blender. 

- Collaborate with peers to create your own VR app. 

- No prior experience required! 

CMPM 17-02: Introduction to Machine Learning: 

- Dive into machine learning fundamentals to build AI models for classification and prediction. 

- Gain hands-on experience with PyTorch, data processing, and neural networks. 

- Basic programming knowledge recommended. 

Why Take These Classes? 

- Gain practical skills through interactive, hands-on learning in a focused and supportive environment.

- Build a project portfolio to jumpstart your career in design and engineering.


Head to the Enrollments tab on My.UCSC.edu and search for CMPM 17 courses under Computational Media to sign up today! Seats are limited and courses are filling up fast, so don’t miss out!


If you need more information on any of the courses, visit our website or reach out with any questions to tfavalor@ucsc.edu.

Have you ever wondered what it’s like to teach your own class? Now’s your chance to become a student professor!

Want to Join CMPM 115: Lead by Design?, keep your potential topic in mind! Through this 5-unit course, upper division students with a range of technical skill sets they are passionate about sharing will be guided through the process of developing a 3 unit engineering project-based design lab. You will then be paid to teach the course to lower division students in a subsequent quarter. Lead by Design requires a team-based application, and is tailored to upper division students.

Applications are now open for Spring Quarter! To have priority, submit by March 8. After this date, positions are approved on a rolling basis.
