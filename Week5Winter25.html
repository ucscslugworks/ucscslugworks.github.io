This week, I spoke with Hayden Krause, a Second Year Astrophysics Major, about his role as a group tutor for Introduction to Rocketry, one of the courses in the Early Design-Build course series (currently being offered as CMPM 17). Hayden discussed the inspiration behind the class, which stemmed from his involvement with Rocket Team (one of the Baskin Engineering Residential Clubs in Slugworks) and his passion for teaching. Hayden highlighted the need for extensive preparation, the lessons learned from teaching, and the importance of maintaining enthusiasm to inspire others.

Q: What is the title of the class you taught?
A: Intro to Rocketry (CMPM 17).

Q: What initially motivated you to join the Lead By Design or First Year Design courses?
A: I’ve always been interested in teaching. I did some tutoring in high school, and I enjoyed it. The inspiration really started when I took a CMPM class on CAD and fabrication design in my first year. It was very collaborative and peer-oriented, and it captured the spirit of Santa Cruz for me. I wanted to create a similar environment and share my passion for rocketry.

Q: Were your expectations for the course different from how the experience turned out?
A: Yes. We had ambitious plans for students to learn CAD, 3D printing, and laser cutting alongside rocketry, but we realized it was better to focus on fewer skills. We scaled back so students could create a final project they were proud of. I also didn’t anticipate how emotionally attached I’d get to the class. It became something I was working on all the time. It was stressful but incredibly fulfilling.

Q: What were the greatest challenges or obstacles during your time teaching?
A: The workload was immense. Lead by Design is a five-unit course, about 15 hours of work a week, but it often felt like more. Writing modules, creating assignments, and preparing labs took a lot of time. Another challenge was classroom management. We didn’t expect to need it at the college level, but we had to learn how to set clearer expectations. Keeping the course on track while balancing other responsibilities was tough.

Q: What advice would you give to new students to set realistic expectations?
A: Prep like crazy. Test every assignment, quiz, and lab. If you can’t build or complete the assignments yourself, your students won’t be able to either. Plan as much as possible beforehand to avoid major changes mid-quarter. Beyond that, stay passionate. Your enthusiasm can inspire your students. Teaching isn’t just about benefiting your career. You need a genuine desire to share knowledge to stay motivated through the tough parts.

Q: Was there enough guidance or resources available to you?
A: There were good resources, especially the pedagogy meetings and feedback from Tela. The focus on pedagogy was valuable, but it sometimes took away from the time we needed to develop the class itself. The feedback was incredibly helpful but could be overwhelming.

Q: What will you remember most about this experience 10 years from now?
A: The final week, when all the students had completed their final projects. I spent 60 hours in the lab helping them build and paint their rockets. Seeing all the completed rockets lined up, each one unique and well-made, was incredibly rewarding.

Q: What would you say to someone considering joining Lead By Design?
A: It’s an amazing opportunity, but you need to know what you’re getting into. Teaching a class is a huge commitment and takes a lot of time, effort, and passion. Be ready for your ideas to change and for your passion to be tested. But if you genuinely care about teaching, it’s incredibly fulfilling.

Q: If you could give one key piece of advice to improve the program itself, what would it be?
A: Clearer communication about the time commitment and expectations for Lead By Design. Students need to understand that this is a major undertaking. A two-quarter structure might help, giving more time for class development. Shifting some focus from pedagogy to the logistics of building and managing a class could also better prepare student group tutors.
